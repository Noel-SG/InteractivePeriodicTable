Hydrogen
Atomic mass: 1.008
Atomic number: 1


Hydrogen is the first element in the periodic table of elements. It is also the lightest, with an atomic mass of 1.008. In its monatomic (H) form, it is the must abundant element in the entire universe.
Helium
Atomic mass: 4.003
Atomic number: 2
1s2


Helium is the second element in the periodic table. It is a noble gas, meaning it is unreactive. As well as hydrogen, it is monatomic. It has the lowest boiling point out of all elements.
